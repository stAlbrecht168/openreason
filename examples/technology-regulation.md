# Example input: technology and regulation framing

## Statement to analyse

> "Technology will solve climate change. We do not need more regulation."

## Context

This type of statement appears in public debate about climate policy — in speeches, interviews, op-eds, and online discussion. The speaker is unspecified. The statement is presented as a self-contained argument about two things: the sufficiency of technological solutions and the dispensability of regulatory approaches.

## Analytical focus

Analyse the **argument structure**, **framing choices**, **rhetorical strategy**, and **discourse patterns** in this statement.

This analysis is not about whether the speaker's empirical claim about technology is correct. It is about what analytical frameworks can reveal about how the argument is constructed, what it leaves out, and what assumptions it requires.

## Out of scope

- Whether any specific technology will or will not solve climate change
- Whether any specific regulatory policy is or is not effective
- The speaker's identity, political affiliation, or intent
